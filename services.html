Core Services

The work takes different forms depending on what you're navigating. Some leaders engage for specific high-stakes decisions. Others find value in ongoing partnership as they build organizations, develop teams, and make choices that shape trajectory over time.

Executive Coaching

One-on-one partnership focused on the challenges in front of you right now. We work through what matters, without preset methodology determining the conversation.

  • Navigating restructuring that carries political complexity
  • Working through team dynamics affecting execution
  • Thinking through career decisions across multiple timeframes
  • Examining patterns you're seeing but haven't fully articulated

Strategic Advisory

Thinking through organizational and business challenges that require both analytical rigor and understanding of human systems.

  • Assessing market entry where timing and approach determine outcomes
  • Designing organizations that enable strategy while remaining sustainable
  • Integrating acquisitions where culture shapes financial performance
  • Building capabilities that create competitive advantage over time

Leadership Transition Support

The first 90 days in a senior role often determine what becomes possible over the following years. We work through what this specific position requires and how to be effective from the outset.

  • Establishing credibility while learning organizational dynamics
  • Making early decisions that shape future possibilities
  • Building relationships that enable execution
  • Reading signals that reveal what this role truly demands

Board Preparation & Development

Preparing for board service or navigating the unique demands of effective board membership. Understanding what distinguishes governance from executive leadership.

  • Providing strategic counsel without executive authority
  • Influencing across different backgrounds and mental models
  • Navigating board dynamics that shape decision quality
  • Developing judgment about when to push and when to listen

Common Situations

High-Stakes Decisions

Decisions where the analytical answer seems clear but organizational reality requires navigation you haven't encountered before. We think through implications, stress-test assumptions, and examine what successful execution actually requires.

  • Acquisitions with compelling logic but substantial integration risk
  • Restructuring that carries political complexity
  • Strategic pivots where timing determines outcomes
  • Investment choices with multi-year implications

Team Effectiveness

These situations resist simple interventions. We work through the human dynamics and organizational factors that determine whether teams perform or underperform their potential.

  • Executive teams with strong individuals but misaligned priorities
  • Talented direct reports who can't translate capability into results
  • Succession planning when the obvious choice has limitations
  • Building trust and candor in high-pressure environments

Organizational Design

Design choices that account for both strategic logic and human realities of how work actually gets done.

  • Restructuring to enable growth without losing effectiveness
  • Integrating acquisitions where culture shapes performance
  • Building matrix structures that work in practice
  • Designing organizations that scale sustainably

Leading Change

Transformation that requires more than communication plans and project management. Creating conditions where change becomes possible rather than mandating compliance that looks like commitment.

  • Understanding where resistance will emerge and why
  • Timing interventions based on organizational readiness
  • Reading signals that determine pacing and approach
  • Building coalitions that enable sustained change

Career Navigation

Career decisions at senior levels have long time horizons and limited room for course correction.

  • Choosing between operational roles that build capability versus strategic roles that position differently
  • Assessing CEO readiness and what development you still need
  • Managing transitions from division to enterprise leadership

Strategy Development

Strategy work that remains grounded in implementation reality throughout. Moving beyond planning cycles that produce documents no one references three months later.

  • Testing strategic options against implementation constraints
  • Building strategies that survive contact with reality
